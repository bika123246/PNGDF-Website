Operations

Current and historical operations of the PNG Defence Force

Border Security Operations

The PNGDF maintains a continuous presence along Papua New Guinea's international borders, with particular focus on the land border with Indonesia (West Papua). Operations include joint patrols combining Land and Air Elements, surveillance monitoring, and community engagement in border regions to maintain security and stability.

Maritime Security Operations

Papua New Guinea's vast Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) — one of the largest in the Pacific — requires constant vigilance. The Maritime Element conducts regular patrols to combat illegal, unreported, and unregulated (IUU) fishing, transnational crime, and to ensure maritime safety. The PNGDF works closely with the National Fisheries Authority and regional partners including Australia, the United States, and Pacific Island Forum nations.

Disaster Response Operations

The PNGDF plays a critical role in disaster response across Papua New Guinea, which is prone to earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, tsunamis, and flooding. The PNGDF Disaster Response Team can deploy rapidly to provide:

  • Search and rescue operations
  • Medical evacuation and emergency healthcare
  • Distribution of relief supplies
  • Engineering support for infrastructure restoration
  • Transport and logistics coordination

Recent notable deployments include response to the 2024 landslide in Enga Province and the 2025 volcanic activity in East New Britain.

International Peacekeeping

The PNGDF has contributed personnel to United Nations peacekeeping missions in various regions, including:

  • UN Peacekeeping Mission in South Sudan (UNMISS)
  • UN Peacekeeping Mission in the Solomon Islands (RAMSI)
  • Bougainville Peace Monitoring Group

These contributions demonstrate PNG's commitment to regional and global stability and provide valuable professional development opportunities for PNGDF personnel.

Nation-Building Tasks

Under the Defence Act, the PNGDF is tasked with contributing to national development. This includes:

  • Infrastructure projects — roads, bridges, airstrips
  • Health and medical outreach programs
  • Engineering and construction support to remote communities
  • Training and capacity building for civilian agencies
