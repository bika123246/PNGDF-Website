About the PNG Defence Force

Our history, mission, and commitment to Papua New Guinea

Our History

The Papua New Guinea Defence Force traces its origins to the Pacific Islands Regiment (PIR), which was formed by the Australian Army during World War II. The PIR served with distinction in the Pacific campaign, particularly during the Kokoda Track campaign and the liberation of Bougainville.

In January 1973, as Papua New Guinea moved toward self-governance, the PNGDF was formally established, inheriting the traditions and assets of the PIR. Following independence on 16 September 1975, the PNGDF became the sovereign military force of the nation, responsible for the defence of Papua New Guinea and its territories.

Over the past five decades, the PNGDF has evolved from a small infantry-based force into a joint military organisation comprising Land, Maritime, and Air Elements, with a personnel strength of approximately 3,600 active members.

Our Mission

The PNGDF is tasked with the following core functions under the Constitution of Papua New Guinea and the Defence Act:

  • Defend Papua New Guinea and its territory from external armed attack
  • Assist in the fulfillment of Papua New Guinea's international obligations, including peacekeeping operations
  • Provide assistance to civilian authorities in meeting civil disasters, restoring public order and security, and addressing national emergencies
  • Contribute to national development tasks, including infrastructure projects and capacity building

Command Structure

The PNGDF is under the control of the National Executive Council (NEC) through the Minister for Defence. The Commander of the PNGDF, currently Rear Admiral Philip Polewara, is the highest-ranking military officer responsible for operational command. Brigadier-General Larry Opa serves as Deputy Chief of Defence Force.

The force is organised into three environmental commands, each with its own commanding officer:

  • Land Element Command
  • Maritime Element Command
  • Air Element Command
