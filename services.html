Advice & Services

Information, guidance, and support services for personnel and the public

Veterans' Affairs

The PNGDF is committed to supporting our veterans and their families. The Veterans' Affairs Office provides advice and assistance on pensions, medical care, counselling services, and welfare support for former PNGDF personnel. Veterans are encouraged to register with the Defence Veterans' Database to ensure they receive information about available benefits and services.

Contact: veterans@defence.gov.pg | Phone: +675 123 4580

Defence Welfare Services

The PNGDF Welfare Service provides support to serving members and their families, including:

  • Family Support: Assistance with housing, relocation, and family services
  • Counselling Services: Professional mental health and wellbeing support
  • Chaplaincy Services: Spiritual and pastoral care for all denominations
  • Financial Advice: Guidance on financial planning and management
  • Health Services: Medical and dental care at PNGDF health facilities

Freedom of Information

Members of the public may request access to information held by the PNGDF under the Freedom of Information Act. Requests must be submitted in writing to the FOI Officer and should specify the information being sought. The PNGDF will respond within 30 days.

Submit FOI requests to: foi@defence.gov.pg

Community Engagement

The PNGDF actively engages with communities across Papua New Guinea through various programs:

  • School Visits: Defence personnel visit schools to discuss career opportunities and the role of the PNGDF
  • Community Projects: Infrastructure and development projects in rural areas
  • Medical Outreach: Health patrols providing basic medical services to remote communities
  • Disaster Preparedness: Community training and awareness programs for natural disasters
