The PNGDF Structure

Understanding our organisation — Land, Maritime, and Air Elements

Organisational Overview

The PNGDF is a joint military force organised into three primary service elements, each with distinct roles and capabilities. The force is commanded from Headquarters PNGDF at Murray Barracks in Port Moresby, with units deployed across Papua New Guinea's four administrative regions.

Land Element

🛡️

The Land Element is the largest component of the PNGDF, responsible for ground-based military operations. It comprises two infantry battalions, an engineer battalion, and support units. Key bases include:

  • Murray Barracks (Port Moresby) — Headquarters
  • Lombrum Naval Base (Manus Province)
  • Moem Barracks (Wewak, East Sepik Province)
  • Igam Barracks (Lae, Morobe Province)
  • Goldie River Training Depot (Central Province)

The Land Element is also heavily involved in nation-building tasks, including infrastructure development, disaster response, and community assistance programs throughout the country.

Maritime Element

⚓

The Maritime Element is the naval arm of the PNGDF, tasked with protecting Papua New Guinea's vast maritime domain. With an Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) of over 3.1 million square kilometres, the Maritime Element plays a crucial role in:

  • Maritime surveillance and patrol operations
  • Combating illegal fishing and transnational crime
  • Search and rescue operations
  • Transport and logistics support
  • International maritime cooperation

Key assets include Guardian-class patrol boats, landing craft, and support vessels. The primary naval base is at Lombrum, Manus Province, with secondary facilities in Port Moresby and other coastal locations.

Air Element

✈️

The Air Element provides aerial capabilities in support of PNGDF operations across all domains. Operating from airfields and airstrips throughout Papua New Guinea, the Air Element is responsible for:

  • Aerial surveillance and reconnaissance
  • Transport of personnel, equipment, and supplies
  • Medical evacuation (MEDEVAC) operations
  • Search and aerial survey missions
  • Support to civil authorities

The Air Element operates a fleet of transport aircraft including the CN235, UH-1H Iroquois helicopters, and other light aircraft. Main operating bases include Jackson Airport (Port Moresby) and forward operating locations across the country.
